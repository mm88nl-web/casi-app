# Casi — Brand Voice Guide

---

## 01 — VOICE OVERVIEW

Casi writes the way a confident founder talks to a streamer they respect. The copy is short because the product is self-evident — long explanations signal insecurity. There's no cheerleading, no buzzwords, no "we believe in creators." Just clean declarative sentences that tell you exactly what the thing does and leave you to decide. The tone is modern and informal but never slang-heavy; it's closer to a well-designed interface than a social media account. Underneath the minimalism there's a dry wit — "Your stream is real estate. Lease it." lands because it's unexpected and accurate, not because it's trying to be funny.

**Voice Essence:** Casi sounds like a confident builder — direct, specific, and a little wry.

---

## 02 — TONE DIMENSIONS

**Formality** — Casual ←●——→ Formal
- Sits casual-left, but not sloppy. Uses contractions. Doesn't use slang.
- In practice: Write the way you'd explain it to a smart friend, not a press release.
- Example: *"The streamer approves every one and keeps 100%."* — not *"Revenue is retained entirely by the content creator."*

**Energy** — Calm ←●——→ Energetic
- Calm. Never exclamation marks. Never hype.
- In practice: Let the product be exciting. The copy just states facts.
- Example: *"Pay-per-minute streamer overlays."* — not *"An exciting new way to monetize your stream!"*

**Humor** — Serious ←——●→ Witty
- Leans slightly witty, but through precision not jokes. The humor comes from an unexpected metaphor that's also technically accurate.
- In practice: One surprising line per page is enough. Don't chase it.
- Example: *"Your stream is real estate. Lease it."*

**Expertise** — Accessible ←——●→ Authoritative
- Specific and technical when it matters (Apache 2.0, Solana, USDC escrow), plain English everywhere else. Never over-explains.
- In practice: Use the right term once. Don't define it. Trust the audience.
- Example: *"Apache 2.0 escrow on Solana."* — trust that streamers who care will look it up.

**Warmth** — Distant ←——●→ Intimate
- Leans intimate but quietly. Achieved through *a viewer's beam, live on stream* — the italic serif does emotional work so the sans-serif copy doesn't have to.
- In practice: Use the serif italic voice for captions, figurative moments, and secondary CTAs. Keep headlines and body copy clean.

## 03 — VOICE QUALITIES

**Short sentences are a feature, not laziness**
The brand's shortest line — *"Get on a live stream."* — is the headline. This is intentional. Density of information per word is a design goal, same as visual minimalism.
- **Do:** *"Viewers pay. Streamers approve. Everyone wins."*
- **Don't:** *"Casi is a platform that enables streamers to monetize their audiences through viewer-funded visual overlays."*

**Specific over vague**
Numbers, formats, and technical details build trust faster than adjectives. If you can say a number, say it. If you can name the technology, name it.
- **Do:** *"55,000+ words. Free, no sign-up."*
- **Don't:** *"Tons of words to choose from."*

**Streamer-first framing**
The streamer is always in control. Copy reflects this — the streamer approves, keeps, decides. Viewer benefits are real but secondary in the hierarchy.
- **Do:** *"The streamer approves every one and keeps 100%."*
- **Don't:** *"Viewers can send beams directly to your stream!"*

**No hype, no hollow adjectives**
Words like "powerful," "seamless," "world-class," "revolutionary," "game-changing" are banned. They say nothing and signal that the product can't speak for itself.
- **Do:** *"Approval-gated. By the minute or per flash."*
- **Don't:** *"A powerful, seamless way to supercharge your stream."*

**Confidence without arrogance**
State what the product does. Don't apologise for what it isn't. Don't oversell what it is.
- **Do:** *"Free or paid. No ads."*
- **Don't:** *"We're just getting started, but we think you'll love what we're building!"*

**The brand name is lowercase**
*casi*, not *Casi* (in body text, UI copy, and social). Matches the wordmark and the design language. Exceptions: start of a sentence, document titles.

## 04 — VOCABULARY

**Words we use:**
- beam, beams (the viewer overlay unit — this is a proper product term)
- stream, on stream, live stream
- studio (the streamer's dashboard/setup)
- approval-gated, approved
- flash (per-flash pricing format)
- per-minute, by the minute
- escrow
- viewers, streamers (not "users," not "creators")
- free tier, paid
- overlay
- earn, keeps (not "monetize" — too corporate)
- wired up, live (for technical states)

**Words we avoid:**
- powerful, seamless, easy, simple (hollow)
- revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive (hype)
- creator (feels generic — "streamer" is more specific)
- users, customers (too transactional — use "streamers" and "viewers")
- monetize (corporate — use "earn" or "get paid")
- engage, engagement (marketing speak)
- leverage, utilize
- solution, platform (too enterprise)
- excited, thrilled, stoked (performative enthusiasm)
- ! (exclamation marks — ever)
- we believe, we think, we're passionate about (self-centered and vague)

**Brand-specific language:**
- **beam** — the viewer overlay that appears on stream (noun and verb: "viewers beam onto your stream")
- **studio** — the streamer's control panel / workspace on casi.gg
- **streamers** and **viewers** — not users, not creators, not fans
- **casi** — lowercase in running copy, wordmark in logo contexts

## 05 — WRITING STYLE RULES

**Sentence length:** Short by default. One idea per sentence. A two-sentence paragraph is fine. Three is a lot. Mix lengths when rhythm demands it — but the short sentence always wins.

**Punctuation:** Em-dash (—) is used as a clean separator in headlines (*casi — viewers pay to appear on your live stream*). Middle dot (·) for stacked specs (*free or paid · approval gated · solana · stripe*). No exclamation marks, ever. Ellipses only in UI loading states.

**Capitalization:** Sentence case for headlines. No title case. *casi* is lowercase even at sentence start in logo/brand contexts; in running prose, capitalise at sentence start.

**Numbers:** Always use numerals (55,000+ not "fifty-five thousand"). Percentages as % (100% not "one hundred percent").

**Contractions:** Use them. *"keeps 100%"* not *"the streamer retains 100% of revenue."*

**Active voice:** Always. No exceptions.

**Humor:** Allowed exactly once per page, in a headline or metaphor position. Must be precise and dry — not a pun, not a wink. The real estate line works because it's a metaphor that's also a technically accurate description of the business model.

## 06 — CHANNEL ADAPTATIONS

| Channel | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Website homepage | Most minimal — let silence do work | *"Get on a live stream."* |
| Product UI / dashboard | Functional, warm, zero fluff | *"No beams waiting for approval."* |
| Error messages | Honest and direct — never apologetic | *"Couldn't connect. Try again."* |
| Social media | Slightly more conversational, still no hype | *"Streamer approves. Viewer appears on stream. That's it."* |
| Email marketing | One clear point per email, serif italic for personality moments | *"Your studio is set up. Time to go live."* |
| SEO / meta descriptions | Specific and factual — keywords earned by accuracy | *"Free, no ads, no sign-up. Classic Klondike in your browser."* |

## 07 — BEFORE AND AFTER EXAMPLES

**Before:** *"Casi is an innovative platform that empowers streamers to unlock new revenue streams through engaging viewer interactions."*
**After:** *"Viewers pay to appear on your stream. You approve each one. You keep everything."*
**Why it works:** Strips the adjectives, puts the streamer in control, three short sentences do the work of one bloated one.

---

**Before:** *"Our easy-to-use tools make it simple to get started in minutes!"*
**After:** *"Set up a studio. Share the link. Go live."*
**Why it works:** Same information, same three steps — but written as instructions, not a promise. No exclamation mark.

---

**Before:** *"We're passionate about helping content creators monetize their audiences."*
**After:** *"Streamers keep 100%. No platform cut."*
**Why it works:** Replaces a feeling (passion) with a fact (100%). The fact is more persuasive.

---

**Before:** *"Join thousands of streamers already using Casi to grow their income!"*
**After:** *"Free or paid. Approval-gated. Works with OBS in five minutes."*
**Why it works:** No false social proof, no urgency pressure. Just three things that are actually true and useful.

---

**Before:** *"Experience the future of live streaming monetization."*
**After:** *"Your stream is real estate. Lease it."*
**Why it works:** The metaphor does the persuasion. "Future of" is meaningless filler.

---

## 08 — THINGS WE NEVER SAY

- *"We're excited to announce…"* — just announce it
- *"Game-changing," "revolutionary," "next-level"* — say what it actually does
- *"Empower," "enable," "unlock"* — hollow verbs
- *"Our community"* — we don't have a cult, we have streamers and viewers
- *"Content creator"* — say streamer
- *"Monetization solution"* — say "get paid"
- *"Seamless experience"* — if it's seamless, it shows; don't say it
- *"Don't hesitate to reach out"* — just say "questions? hi@casi.gg"
- Any sentence ending in *!*
- Referring to casi in the third person in copy aimed at users ("Casi helps you…") — use "you" and the direct frame
