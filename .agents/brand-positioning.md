# Casi — Brand Positioning

---

## 01 — CATEGORY DEFINITION

**Stated category:** Streamer monetization tools

**Actual category:** When a streamer makes a decision, they're comparing casi against donation/tip tools (Streamlabs, StreamElements) and deciding whether to add another thing to their stream setup. That's the real consideration set — not "overlay tools," not "streaming platforms."

**Opportunity category:** Live audience interaction — the frame where viewers don't just pay, they *appear*. This is a category casi could own outright, because no one else is in it. The competition is all playing in the same "tipping" territory; casi is doing something structurally different (visual, on-screen, approval-gated).

**Recommendation:** Frame casi as **live audience interaction**, not streamer monetization. Monetization is the mechanism; interaction is the value. "Viewers pay to appear on your stream" is already correct — double down on that frame. It creates a category where casi has no direct competition.

---

## 02 — COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**Axis 1:** Passive (viewer sends money → gets a notification) ← → Active (viewer takes action → appears on screen)
**Axis 2:** Streamer has no control ← → Streamer has full control

**Streamlabs / StreamElements** — Top-left. Passive + low control. A donation happens, a TTS notification fires. The streamer is a conduit, not a gatekeeper. These tools are ubiquitous, free, and interchangeable with each other.

**Ko-fi / Buy Me a Coffee** — Far-left, passive. Tipping mechanics with no live integration. The streamer finds out about it after the fact, often via email.

**Twitch Bits / Channel Points** — Passive + moderate control. Platform-native, so there's no setup, but the streamer is locked into Twitch's feature set. No visual overlay, no custom content.

**casi** — Top-right. **Active + full control.** The viewer's content appears live on screen, visually. The streamer approves every single one. Nothing happens without the streamer's say-so. This quadrant is empty.

**The unclaimed territory:** No competitor sits in Active + Full Control. That's casi's position.

---

## 03 — POSITIONING TERRITORY

**The Space:** casi owns *streamer-controlled, live visual interaction* — the only tool where a viewer's action appears on screen, exactly as the streamer decides.

**The Audience Owned:** Streamers on Twitch and Kick who want a new way to make viewers feel like part of the show — without giving up control of what their stream looks like.

**The Competitive Moat:**
Three things make this position hard to copy. First, the approval-gate is a product philosophy, not just a feature — building a tool for streamers who care about control attracts a fundamentally different audience than tools built around frictionless tipping. Second, the dual payment rails (Solana escrow + Stripe) mean casi works for crypto-native streamers *and* everyone else, without forcing a choice. Third, the open-source escrow (Apache 2.0 on Solana) is a trust signal that platforms like Streamlabs can never replicate — they are the platform.

**Strategic (internal):**
*"casi owns the Active + Full Control quadrant of live streaming interaction — the only product where viewer-paid visual content hits the stream on the streamer's terms, not the platform's."*

**Conversational (for landing page, social):**
*"Viewers pay to get on your stream. You decide what makes the cut."*

---

## 05 — POSITIONING PROOF POINTS

- **Streamer approves every beam** — nothing appears on stream without explicit approval. This is a hard product guarantee, not a setting.
- **Open-source escrow on Solana** — funds are held in an Apache 2.0 smart contract, not in casi's bank account. The money mechanics are auditable by anyone.
- **Dual payment rails** — viewers pay with Solana (USDC) or a card (Stripe). The streamer doesn't have to choose a side.
- **OBS browser source** — zero proprietary software. If you use OBS, the overlay works. No download, no plugin.
- **Streamer keeps 100%** — casi takes no cut of beam revenue (monetised via the platform itself, not a rake on every transaction).
- **Free tier** — free beams exist. The tool works without any money changing hands, which lowers the barrier for new streamers.

---

## 06 — WHAT CASI REFUSES TO BE

- **casi is not for streamers who want fire-and-forget tips.** If you want a donation button with a TTS sound, Streamlabs exists. casi is for streamers who want control over what happens on their screen.
- **casi will never compete on notification design.** Streamlabs and StreamElements have spent years making alert boxes more animated. casi doesn't own that territory and doesn't want it.
- **casi does not try to be a streaming platform.** casi works *with* Twitch, Kick, and YouTube — it doesn't replace them or try to host streams.
- **casi is not a creator marketplace.** There's no discover feed, no algorithmic ranking of streamers. casi is infrastructure, not a destination.

---

## 07 — POSITIONING IN ONE SENTENCE

Casi is the only live streaming tool where viewers pay to appear on screen — and the streamer approves every single one.

---

## ⚠ Positioning Red Flags to Watch

- **"Streamer monetization" framing is too broad** — it puts casi in a category with Patreon, Twitch subscriptions, and merch stores. The positioning wins by going narrower: live visual interaction.
- **"Free" as a differentiator won't hold** — free tiers exist everywhere. Lead with control and interaction, not price.
- **Don't let the Solana angle dominate** — it's a proof point (open escrow, trust), not the positioning. Leads who filter on "crypto tool" are a narrower audience than leads who filter on "streamer control."
